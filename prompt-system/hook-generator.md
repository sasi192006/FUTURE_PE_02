# Hook Generator Prompt  
## UGC Paid Ads – D2C Skincare

---

## Purpose

This prompt is designed to generate high-performing, scroll-stopping hooks for D2C skincare paid ads.

Hooks are the most critical part of short-form ad performance.  
The goal is to create pattern interrupts that immediately:

- Call out a pain point
- Spark curiosity
- Promise a benefit
- Trigger emotional relevance

These hooks are built specifically for paid advertising (Reels, TikTok Ads, Shorts).

---

# Hook Generation Prompt

Use the structure below to generate hooks.

---

You are a performance marketing strategist creating scroll-stopping hooks for paid UGC ads in the D2C skincare industry.

Generate high-converting hooks based on audience psychology and skincare pain points.

---

## Input Details

Product Category:
Target Audience:
Primary Skin Concern:
Core Benefit:
Unique Selling Proposition (USP):
Tone Direction (Relatable / Bold / Emotional / Informative):

---

## Hook Requirements

Generate 30 hooks.

Rules:

- Maximum 12 words
- Written for spoken delivery (natural tone)
- No corporate language
- No unrealistic promises
- Clear emotional or benefit-driven angle
- Designed for first 3 seconds of a video ad
- Suitable for paid advertising

---

## Hook Categories (Mandatory Distribution)

Divide hooks into the following categories:

### 1. Problem-Led Hooks (8 hooks)

Focus on pain points.

Examples of direction:
- Acne frustration
- Dull skin
- Pigmentation
- Wasted money on products
- Sensitive skin struggles

These should immediately identify with the viewer’s problem.

---

### 2. Curiosity Hooks (6 hooks)

Create intrigue without revealing everything.

Should make the viewer think:
- “Wait, what?”
- “Why?”
- “I need to know more.”

Avoid vague clickbait — keep it relevant to skincare.

---

### 3. Direct Benefit Hooks (6 hooks)

Clearly state the transformation or outcome.

Focus on:
- Glow
- Clear skin
- Even tone
- Confidence

Keep it specific and realistic.

---

### 4. POV Hooks (5 hooks)

Write in POV format.

Examples of direction:
- POV: You finally find a serum that works
- POV: Your skin stops breaking out

These perform well on Instagram and TikTok.

---

### 5. Objection-Based Hooks (5 hooks)

Target skepticism.

Examples of direction:
- “I don’t trust skincare ads, but…”
- “I thought this wouldn’t work…”

These reduce resistance early in the ad.

---

## Performance Criteria

Each hook must:

- Capture attention instantly
- Mention a pain, benefit, or emotional trigger
- Sound like a real person speaking
- Avoid technical ingredient explanations
- Avoid generic phrases like “best product ever”

---

## Output Format

Organize clearly under category headings.
