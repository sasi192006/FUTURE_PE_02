## UGC Narrative Ads for D2C Skincare Brands

---

## Purpose

This prompt generates high-converting storytelling-style UGC ads for D2C skincare brands.

Unlike PAS or BAB, this framework focuses on:

- Emotional relatability
- Personal journey
- Authenticity
- Trust-building through narrative

This format performs especially well for:
- Cold audiences
- Brand-building campaigns
- Retargeting
- Founder-led ads
- Customer testimonial creatives

Ideal duration: 30–60 seconds.

---

# Storytelling Script Generation Prompt

The tone must feel real, not promotional.

# Framework Structure (Mandatory)

Follow this sequence:

---

## 1. Relatable Opening (Hook)

- Start with a moment, not a pitch.
- Use a conversational tone.
- Sound like someone talking to a friend.
- Immediately hint at the struggle.

The first 3 seconds must create curiosity or emotional connection.

Example style direction:
“I wasn’t even planning to talk about this, but…”

---

## 2. The Struggle Phase

- Describe the real experience.
- Mention failed attempts.
- Share emotional impact (confidence, social anxiety, frustration).
- Keep it believable.

Avoid exaggeration.

Make the audience feel understood.

---

## 3. Turning Point

- Introduce the moment of discovery.
- Keep it natural.
- No dramatic sales tone.
- Explain why you decided to try it.

This should feel like a genuine shift, not a forced product plug.

---

## 4. The Result

- Share changes noticed.
- Focus on feeling + visible improvements.
- Mention timeframe carefully (no guarantees).
- Address one doubt or objection.

Keep it subtle and real.

---

## 5. Soft Close + CTA

- Encourage action gently.
- Suggest rather than push.
- Align CTA with campaign goal.

Example direction:
“If you’re dealing with the same thing, maybe try it.”

# Performance Rules

- Must feel unscripted
- Avoid brand-heavy tone
- Keep under 60 seconds
- Mention one differentiator (USP)
- Address one objection subtly
- Avoid ingredient overload
- Avoid unrealistic transformation claims
- Keep language simple and conversational

---

# Platform Adaptation

Instagram Reels:
- Clean, aesthetic visuals
- Emotional but confident delivery

TikTok:
- Raw, casual, more personality-driven
- Slight pauses and natural speech patterns

YouTube Shorts:
- Slightly more structured
- Clear narrative arc
- Confident ending

---

# Restrictions

Do NOT:

- Promise permanent results
- Use medical terminology
- Claim guaranteed transformation
- Sound like a scripted commercial
- Use exaggerated emotional drama

The final script must feel like:
A real person sharing something personal — not an ad.
